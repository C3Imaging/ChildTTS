Implementation and Initial Evaluation of TTS Pipeline and Workflow for Practical Child Speech Synthesis.

ABSTRACT:

Speech Synthesis has come a long way as current Text-To-Speech (TTS) models can now generate natural human-sounding speech. However, one trend that’s been seen is that most of the TTS research focuses on using adult speech data and there has been very limited work done with child speech synthesis, primarily due to lack of child speech data available for research use. In this study, the benefit of finetuning adult speech-trained TTS models on small child speech datasets using a speaker verification- based Multi speaker TTS approach via transfer learning is demonstrated. A publicly available child speech dataset was cleaned to produce a smaller subset of approximately 19 hours and used in finetuning. Both subjective and objective evaluation were performed on our results. We used a pretrained MOSNet for performing the objective evaluation and we propose a MOS-like subjective evaluation method specifically for synthetic child speech, which is relatively easier to perform, and saves time and resources as compared to other Mushra-based and Crowd sourcing MOS evaluation methods. Our subjective evaluation achieved the MOS of 3.92 for ‘Speech Intelligibility’ and 3.85 for ‘Voice Naturalness’ and further discussion on other sub-categories of subjective evaluation is presented in the paper. Our trained model was able to synthesize child-like speech with reference audio as small as 5 seconds.
